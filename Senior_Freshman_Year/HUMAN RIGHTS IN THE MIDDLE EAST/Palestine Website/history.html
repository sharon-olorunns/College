Palestine

Is an occupied Arab country, located in the continent of Asia, it’s special for the nature of its fertile land, and olive trees everywhere, and is the beginning of many of the past civilizations. With it being the start of many of civilizations in the past and having religions all met in its land, Judaism, Christianity and Islam, it was one of the factors that got many leaders’ eyes into wanting to occupy it. Palestine is now divided in its territory because of the Zionist occupation and the establishment of the State of Israel in the occupied territories, which led to the displacement of its indigenous population within it, or getting Palestinians scattered abroad. To bring together Jews from all around the world to live in it.

Factors that helped with initiating the State of Israel

The British Occupation

The Israelites coexisted with Palestinians without showing any kind of hatred towards them. With WWI coming to an end, England occupied Palestine, taking it under their imperialist rule.

Herzl, the founder of the Zionist movement, shared his ideas about having one home, one land that gathers all Jews together from around the world in his book “The Zionist State”. During the British occupation on Palestine the Foreign Minister Arthur Balfour got affected by these ideas and decided to give Jews their own state, to get rid of their large influx into Britain and European countries, so he started to think of many destinations, one from them being a state in Africa, but it did not match with what Herzl had described in his book, so Balfour started to think of a state that’s closer in description to Herzl’s Zionism ideas and what’s mentioned in the Old Testament, the perfect destination to be the National State of all Jews.

The Zionist Occupation

In 1948, the British forces started to withdraw from the Palestinian land, they evacuated all the places they were present at to hand it to the Zionist army, along with all the ammunition and weapons to control the land on which they were able to seize without much trouble and to expand their deployment in Palestine.

And so they declared the establishment of State of Israel on the Palestinian territories on 15th May, 1948.

Al-Nakba

As soon as the State of Israel got declared, the 1948 war started, called Al-Nakba. The Zionist army forced millions of Palestinians out of their homes, they committed shootings, bombings, massive massacres even before and after the date of declaration such as Balad al-Shaykh massacre, Deir Yassin massacre, Ein al-Zeitun massacre, Abu Shusha, Al-Dawayima massacre and many more.

They destroyed and abolished many Palestinian villages, they occupied the rest and settled/lived in it. Some Arab forces tried to address, get in and fight against this huge chaos and this disgusting occupation but they could not do much due to the Zionist army being equipped with more efficient weapons than what the Arab armies used.

Al-Naksa

As the villages and the cities they destroyed were not enough for this Zionist attack, the only way to get more was obviously by expanding the Israeli settlements by destroying more. And with that the war of Al-Naksa in 1967 started between the Arab army (Jordan, Egypt, Syria) and Israel.

Unfortunately it ended up with Israel being able to defeat the Arab army due to their huge collection of weapons and of course the huge support they got from England and America. And like that, they succeeded at occupying more parts of Palestine. With negotiations with the bigger Arab countries, Palestinians were able to take back these parts that were taken except Plateau the Golan Heights in Syria and the Shebaa Farms in Lebanon, which they still do occupy to this day.
