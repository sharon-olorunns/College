Siege on Gaza Strip

The Gaza Strip is treated as a war zone for testing weapons and ammunition which is why you do not really see any israeli settlements placed in or near there. The Israeli army has killed more than 2,000 Palestinian civilians in the last 3 conflicts:

      ‐ Operation Cast Lead: Dec 27, 2008 – Jan 18, 2009 (23 days)

      ‐ Operation Pillar of Defense: Nov 14, 2012 – Nov 21, 2012 (7 days)

      ‐ Operation Protective Edge: Jul 8, 2014 – Aug 26, 2014 (48 days)

These attacks violate human rights to do the targeting of civilians and civilian structures classifying them as war crimes. Authorities have previously indicted only three soldiers, though in january of 2017 a Israeli military court convicted Elor Azaria, a soldier whose execution of a wounded palestinian, was caught on video. His conviction and 18 month prison sentences were both reduced by the military chief of staff in September. Largely these war crimes have not been pursued and the people responsible for them walk free.
